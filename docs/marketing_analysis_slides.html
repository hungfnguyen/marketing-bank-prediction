1. Problem Overview

Context

In the banking sector, approaching and convincing customers to subscribe to term deposits is a major challenge.

Challenges

  • Telemarketing campaigns have low response rates
  • Not every customer is interested in the product
  • Campaign operating costs are high

Core Problem

Predict the likelihood that a customer will subscribe to a term deposit based on personal information, campaign history, and macroeconomic indicators.

2/11

2. Research Questions

Question 1

Which customers are most likely to accept the term deposit offer?

Question 2

Which factors (personal characteristics, marketing campaign attributes, or economic context) have the strongest impact on the decision?

Question 3

Can we build a predictive model to help the bank optimize the marketing campaign, reduce unnecessary calls, and improve business outcomes?

3/11

3. Research Data

Dataset: Bank Marketing - Additional Full

Source: UCI Machine Learning Repository

Size: 41,188 records

Variables: 20 input features + 1 target variable

Origin: Portuguese bank

Period: 2008-2010

Collection method: Telephone survey

Feature Groups

  • Personal information: age, occupation, marital status, education, credit default
  • Contact information: communication channel, call time, call duration
  • Campaign information: number of contacts, days since last contact
  • Economic indicators: employment rate, CPI, EURIBOR rate
  • Target variable (y): yes/no - subscribed to the term deposit
4/11

4. Input - Explanatory Variables

Personal Information

  • age: age
  • job: occupation
  • marital: marital status
  • education: education level
  • default: credit in default
  • housing: housing loan
  • loan: personal loan

Contact & Campaign

  • contact: communication type
  • month: contact month
  • day_of_week: contact day
  • duration: call duration
  • campaign: number of calls in current campaign
  • pdays: days since previous contact
  • previous: number of previous contacts
  • poutcome: outcome of previous campaign

Macroeconomic Indicators

  • emp.var.rate, cons.price.idx, cons.conf.idx, euribor3m, nr.employed
5/11

5. Output & Evaluation Metrics

Output (Target Variable - Y)

y: Final outcome for each customer

  • yes: Accepted the term deposit offer
  • no: Declined or not interested

Evaluation Metrics (Binary Classification)

  • Accuracy: Overall correct prediction rate
  • Precision: Correctness of positive predictions
  • Recall: Coverage/sensitivity
  • F1-score: Harmonic mean (important for imbalanced data)
  • ROC-AUC: Ability to distinguish between classes

6. Proposed Methods (1/2)

Baseline Models

Logistic Regression: Easy to interpret, captures linear relationships

Decision Tree: Intuitive, uncovers decision rules

Advanced Models

  • Random Forest: Ensemble of trees that reduces overfitting
  • Gradient Boosting (XGBoost): High performance on imbalanced data
  • Neural Networks: Capture complex nonlinear relationships
